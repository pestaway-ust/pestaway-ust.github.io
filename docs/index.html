Pestaway: A Pest Management Chatbot with Integrated Speech Capability

A bilingual conversational assistant that integrates speech input/output to help Filipino farmers identify pests, learn safe mitigation approaches, and access pest management information in English and Tagalog.

üìÑ Abstract

Farmers faced challenges with pest control, leading to lower crop yields and financial losses due to limited access to accurate information. To address this, the study introduced Pestaway, a chatbot for Filipino farmers that uses Large Language Models (LLMs) to provide interactive and speech-enabled solutions. An 80,000-item dataset divided between English and Tagalog was generated using GPT-4 and credible agricultural sources.

The models were fine-tuned using different training approaches: Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), SFT with Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), and Odds Ratio Preference Optimization (ORPO). Among them, the ORPO Trainer produced the best results, achieving a METEOR score of 0.3271, a BERTScore of 0.7316, and an average perplexity of 1.5185.

Additionally, word error rate (WER) and character error rate (CER) were used to evaluate the speech recognition capabilities of the chatbot. The WER ranged from 0% to 28.57%, while the CER ranged from 0% to 3.43%, indicating accurate transcriptions with minimal character-level errors. A user satisfaction survey involving farmers, agricultural students, and individuals interested in agricultural practices yielded an average rating of more than 4.1 out of 5, with 97.73% giving positive feedback and 100% recommending the chatbot for use.

üîç System Overview

Pestaway is a conversational assistant that integrates speech input/output to help users quickly identify household and agricultural pests, learn safe mitigation approaches, and log actions. The pipeline follows a clear progression: detection & knowledge lookup ‚Üí dialogue management ‚Üí speech synthesis. The system is designed for modest hardware and field deployment.

üéØ Key Features

  • Bilingual support (English and Tagalog)
  • Speech-to-Text using Whisper
  • Text-to-Speech using Google TTS (gTTS)
  • Fine-tuned SeaLLM-v3-1.5B-Chat model
  • GUI developed using Gradio

üó£Ô∏è Speech Integration

The chatbot uses Whisper, a speech recognition model designed to perform multilingual speech recognition, translation, and language identification; and Google Text-to-Speech (gTTS) to allow users to transform written text into audio. This enables hands-free operation, particularly useful during fieldwork when typing is inconvenient.

üìä Dataset Generation

Three datasets were generated for training the chatbot, each serving a specific purpose in the fine-tuning process:

  • Raw Dataset: Collected from government websites, journals, and credible sources for the SFT dataset foundation
  • SFT Dataset: 80,000 question-and-answer pairs (40,000 English, 40,000 Tagalog) generated using GPT-4
  • DPO Dataset: 80,000 samples with prompts, chosen, and rejected responses to align with human preferences
  • Ground Truth Dataset: 500 items verified by agricultural experts from the Institute of Weed Science, Entomology, and Plant Pathology (IWEP)

üß† Model Training

The researchers used SeaLLM-v3-1.5B-Chat, a multilingual model that supports various languages including English and Tagalog. The model balances performance and efficiency while using less VRAM and training time. Three training approaches were evaluated:

  • Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT): Develops a pre-trained model using labeled data
  • SFT + Direct Preference Optimization (DPO): Refines model to align outputs with human preferences
  • Odds Ratio Preference Optimization (ORPO): Aligns language models with human preferences without requiring a reference model
